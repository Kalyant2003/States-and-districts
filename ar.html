* Arunachal Pradesh is a state in northeast India.
* It was formed from the North-East Frontier Agency (NEFA) region, and India declared it as a state on 20 February 1987.
* Itanagar is its capital and largest town.
* It borders the Indian states of Assam and Nagaland to the south.
* It shares international borders with Bhutan in the west, Myanmar in the east, and a disputed 1,129 km border with China's Tibet Autonomous Region in the north at the McMahon Line.
* Arunachal Pradesh is claimed by China as part of the Tibet Autonomous Region; China occupied some regions of Arunachal Pradesh in 1962 but later withdrew its forces.
