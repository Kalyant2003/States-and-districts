* Anjaw District is an administrative district in the state of Arunachal Pradesh in north-east India.
* It was created district in 2004, by splitting off from the Lohit district under the Arunachal Pradesh Re-organization of Districts Amendment Act.
* The district borders China on the north. Hawai, at an altitude of 1296 m above sea level, is the district headquarters, located on the banks of the Lohit River, a tributary of the Brahmaputra River.
* It is the easternmost district in India.
* The furthest villages towards the border with China are Dong, Walong, Kibithu and Kaho. Landscape of Anjaw.
* Anjaw is the second least populous district in India (out of 640)
